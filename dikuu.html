❤ Letter One

How Much I Love You

Babu, I have tried a hundred times to explain this feeling — and every time, words feel too small. Too ordinary. Like trying to hold the ocean in a paper cup. But I will try again, because you deserve to hear it.

I love you in the way that is quiet and permanent. Not the loud, dramatic kind that burns bright for a season and disappears. The kind that sits in my chest like a second heartbeat. The kind that makes me think of you when something makes me laugh, when something makes me sad, when something makes me wonder — because you are the first person my mind runs to, every single time.

You are not just someone I am with, Diya. You are the person who changed the shape of my life. Before you, I was existing. With you, I am living. You brought warmth into corners of me I had given up on. You made me feel things I had convinced myself I was not capable of feeling.

I love the way you think. The way you laugh when something genuinely catches you. The way you care — deeply, quietly, without making a show of it. I love the version of you that the world sees, and I love even more the version only I get to see — the soft, unguarded one. The one you only let through when you feel safe.

You are my favourite part of every day, Babu. Even the days we spend apart — you are still there, woven into everything I do. And I just need you to know that this love I carry for you is not a passing thing. It is not going anywhere. It is yours — completely, entirely, forever.

— Always, Your Babu

🙏 Letter Two

I Am So Sorry

This letter is the hardest one to write. Not because I don't mean it — but because I mean it so completely, and I am afraid that words alone will not be enough to show you that.

Babu, I have not always been the person you deserved. There were moments I was too proud, too stubborn, too careless with your feelings. Moments I said things I cannot take back. Moments I chose my ego over your heart — and those moments haunt me, because you never deserved any of it.

I am sorry for every time I made you cry when all you wanted was to be understood. I am sorry for every time I went cold when you needed warmth. For every argument that could have been avoided if I had just listened — really listened — instead of waiting to be right.

I am sorry for the times you needed me and I wasn't fully there. For the moments I took your patience for granted, not realising how much courage it takes to keep loving someone through their worst. You gave me that courage over and over again. I did not always honour it the way I should have.

I want you to know that every wrong thing I did came from a place of being broken, not from a place of not loving you enough. I have always loved you — even in my worst moments. But I know that love without effort means nothing. And so I am not just saying sorry with these words.

I am saying sorry with the promise that I am trying to be better. Every single day. For you. Because you are worth every bit of the effort it takes to be the person you deserve to be loved by.

Please forgive me, Babu. Not because I deserve it — but because my heart will never stop asking for it.

— Yours, Broken but Trying

🌙 Letter Three

You Are All I Have

Diya, I need you to understand something that I have never been able to say out loud without my voice breaking.

I don't have anyone else. Not the way I have you.

I have people around me — faces, names, conversations. But you are the only person in my life who has ever made me feel like I am not alone in this world. The only one I can be completely honest with. The only one I trust with the real version of me — the afraid one, the tired one, the one that doesn't have everything figured out.

When the world feels like too much, you are the only person I want to run to. When I am happy, you are the first person I want to tell. When I am hurting, you are the only one whose voice makes it quieter. That is not a small thing, Babu. That is everything.

I know I don't always show this the way I should. Sometimes I pull away when I should hold on. Sometimes I go quiet when I should speak. But please know that my silence is never emptiness — it is always filled with you. With how much I need you. With how terrified I am of a life without you in it.

You are my home. My only real one. The one person who makes this world feel like a place worth staying in. And I am writing this because I need you to carry that with you — on the days you doubt me, on the days you wonder if you matter to me — you matter more than I have the words to say.

You are not just part of my life, Diya. You are the reason I have one worth living. Please never forget that. Please never leave.

— Yours, Completely and Only
